 # AI Research Daily - 2026-07-16 (Digest)

 ## Today's highlights

 **Win by Silence: Plan Evaluators Reward Omission.** Proves deletion non-monotonicity in expected-value plan scoring: every route in a 26-route cohort had at least one score-improving deletion. GATE typed-state gating neutralizes omission splices before score release. This is a structural property of evaluation architectures, not a model-tuning bug. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12986)

 **Resist and Update: Activation-Level Alignment.** LLMs misreport under pressure while internal belief is unchanged. Causal interchange interventions identify low-rank report coordinates for answer, confidence, and caveat that are independently controllable. A two-pass counterfactual clamp achieves 1.00 resist+update (95% CI [0.99,1.00]). Transfers to SycophancyEval. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12985)

 **Knowledgeless LLMs: Entity-Anonymized Pretraining.** Suppressing entity-linked supervision during pretraining (by anonymizing named entities) produces models that reason from evidence rather than parametric memory. 20-25% relative gains on retrieval-grounded QA with imperfect evidence. Better calibration and abstention. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12831)

 **Agent Evaluation Cracking at Every Layer.** Three papers converge: MemOps deconstructs memory into lifecycle operations exposing hidden failures [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12893); Who Grades the Grader co-evolves metrics and skills, retaining 88-110% of ground-truth lift [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12790); OAT achieves 200-5000x faster failure attribution trained on only 100 successful trajectories [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12747).

 **Extractable Memorization From First Principles.** Fixes symmetric validity problems in memorization measurement. Matched comparison with conformal tests shows prior work inflated extraction rates (non-training false positives accounted for 24% of claims) while simultaneously understating memorization risk for books (thresholds as low as 1e-27). [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12649)

 **GRPO Null Result at 4-8B Scale.** Controlled 18-run grid finds GRPO reinforcement learning does not credibly improve agent success rate over a strong supervised baseline — and makes it worse at higher learning rates. The finding held across both text and vision-language web agent tasks. [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12640)
